The hard part · 04

Today's technology gets you 90% to production-grade 4D. That last 10% is a gold mine and a narrow window of opportunity.

Turning an image into a static shape is basically solved. TRELLIS, Hunyuan3D and TripoSG produce clean geometry that's 80–95% accurate from a single picture.89 The moment you need motion, the frontier breaks.

Where it breaks

No shared topology

Today's 4D models output clouds of Gaussians or a brand-new mesh every frame. There's no consistent topology across time, so nothing to rig, retarget, or load into a game engine.11 A beautiful blob is still a blob.

Why it's possible now

The technical breakthrough just arrived

Meta's ActionMesh (Jan 2026) showed feed-forward, rig-free, topology-consistent animated meshes are achievable.10 MORPHOS (Jun 2026) achieved SOTA for long-horizon animations.15

Temporial = one image → production-grade 4D

Everyone else is waiting to perfect that last 10% — the clean rig, the consistent topology, the engine-ready output. We don't wait. We sit at the frontier of the research and productize it: the pipeline, the product and the platform that get it in front of customers willing to use it before it's perfect. The giants won't race us for this — they sell compute and foundation models, not asset pipelines, and the layer is still too small to move their numbers. That's the opening, and it's roughly 12 to 18 months before it gets crowded. That's our golden window.

The counter-intuitive part

Even generative 2D video is starting to run on 3D.

The loudest gold rush in AI right now is text-to-video — Seedance, Veo, Kling, Runway. On the surface it looks like the opposite of what we do. It isn't. These models produce gorgeous footage but can't be directed: no reliable camera, no blocking, no shot-to-shot continuity. The fix the field is converging on is to drive the model with rough 3D previz as the control track.

The new workflow

Previz becomes the steering wheel

Block a scene in rough 3D, then use it to steer a generative model into stylized, controllable previews — choosing how literally the AI follows the 3D, from coarse camera motion to frame-accurate character action.17 The 3D scene stops being the final render and becomes the control signal.

Why it's our lane

That control layer is our output

Animated, riggable, topology-consistent 3D from a single image is exactly what Temporial produces — and exactly what a directed AI shot needs underneath it. The technology that looks like 3D's replacement turns out to be one of its largest customers.

Generative video doesn't compete with the 4D engine — it plugs into it

The biggest gold rush of all needs animated 3D in a less obvious place: not as the thing on screen, but as the rig steering what ends up on screen. One more surface where every directed shot is an animated 3D asset someone has to make — and we make it in minutes.
